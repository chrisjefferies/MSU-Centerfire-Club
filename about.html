Membership benefits

Membership includes a variety of perks with partner organizations that support the shooting sports in the Mid-Michigan area.

At Looking Glass River Sportsman's Club CENTERFIRE membership provides:

  • No range fees to outdoor, centerfire 50, 75, 100 and 200 yard ranges, trap range, or indoor 20 yard small bore range at all club practices and events.
  • Club Member fee rates at all Looking Glass events.
  • Upgrade to full Looking Glass membership at a discounted rate and get 24/7 access, including summer.

At Chief Okemos Sportsman's Club CENTERFIRE membership provides:

  • Regular participation in invitational handgun steel plate shoots.

At Alpha Dog Customs CENTERFIRE membership provides:

  • Flat 10% discount on Firearms, Ammo and Accessories. Does not include services.
  • Club pricing on club purchases like bulk 5.56, 9mm, and equipment.

We are constantly working to expand member benefits to include more local organizations and businesses that support the shooting sports.

The best pro2a club on campus

MSU CENTERFIRE is dedicated to supporting recreational and competitive shooting sports in every way we can. From training and coaching, to events and competitions, members get access to the best firearms resources available.

Members who are interested in competition are encouraged to supply their own firearms. The club will help you refine your technique, get you access to ammo at rock bottom prices, and will provide things like eye/ear protection, holsters and accessories. In addition, the firearm experts at partner businesses like Alpha Dog Customs are always ready to advise, or facilitate, any equipment purchases.

I am an international student. Specifically, a Nonimmigrant Alien on a Student Visa. Can I still possess firearms as a non-citizen?

Good question. The reality is... it's complicated, and we're not lawyers.

Here is something to read up on if you're interested in buying your own firearms, legally. Basically, nonimmigrant aliens are on the same prohibition list as felons, and can only buy firearms if they meet certain waiver conditions, one of which seems to be possessing a valid hunting permit, and having lived here for 90 days. ATF Nonimmigrant Alien FAQ.

So if you want to buy a firearm, great. You can join the club, and we can get you into hunter's safety courses.

According to Michigan law, lawful aliens may also obtain a Concealed Pistol License, if they've been a resident for six months.

Okay, so that's how I buy firearms. How about if I just want to come out and shoot them for a while?

Well, it looks like there's no legal difference between possession as owning one more or less permanently, and just holding or shooting one.

18 U.S.C. §922(g):
(g) It shall be unlawful for any person—
...
(5) who, being an alien—
...
(B) except as provided in subsection (y)(2), has been admitted to the United States under a nonimmigrant visa (as that term is defined in section 101(a)(26) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. 1101 (a)(26)));
... to ship or transport in interstate or foreign commerce, or possess in or affecting commerce, any firearm or ammunition; or to receive any firearm or ammunition which has been shipped or transported in interstate or foreign commerce.
Full Statute

It seems to be that the statute prohibits possession by aliens, unless the firearm itself is outside the authority of the Federal Government under the Commerce Clause of the US Constitution (i.e. it has never crossed state lines, or affected commerce across state lines. Despite the original intent of the commerce clause, basically any firearm can be construed as falling under the authority of commerce clause today).

Until we get legal advice saying differently, our policy is that any MSU student who we know to be a nonimmigrant alien must be otherwise legally allowed to buy a firearm before we let them shoot one of ours.

Is this club a good place to participate in political advocacy on 2nd Amendment issues or other issues?

No. In order to best support young shooters, this organization refrains from political advocacy. The people you want to talk to are the NRA, NRA-ILA, and Friends of NRA, Michigan. The MSU CENTERFIRE club supports preserving 2nd Amendment freedoms for future generations, and we feel we can best do this by teaching new shooters, and providing training and education to the student community.

Do I need any firearms experience to join?

Not at all! If joining this club seems like something you'd like to try, feel free to attend any one of our many events that are open to non-members, or ask about attending one of our members-only events for a small fee (usually a range fee and the cost of ammo).

Firearms, ammo and some accessories aren't allowed on campus. Where is equipment stored?

All of our equipment is stored safely and securely off-campus at Looking Glass River Sportsman's Club. Club members can store their personal firearms and equipment with the club for free, or as per MSU policy, with the MSU Police Department. In accordance with the law and University policy, all club affiliated firearms, ammo and accessories never come onto campus.

I'd like to learn more about shooting, but I'm not really interested in any competitions. Is this still a good club for me?

Absolutely! The MSU CENTERFIRE club has some great resources available for strictly recreational members, including (often free) equipment rental and discounts at area businesses and ranges. Club members also get the benefit of being in touch with others who share your interest, and can join in on-campus pizza and billiards nights to meet new friends and talk guns. Recreationally-inclined members are welcome attend casual clay pigeon, and steel plate pistol shoots, which are a blast. Of course, practices geared toward competition are always open to all members, as well.

So... MSU CENTERFIRE Club, MSU Rifle and Pistol Club... What's the deal?

We're the same entity. Since its inception as the Marksmanship Club, this shooting sports organization has been providing students an outlet for the safe use of firearms.

The MSU Rifle and Pistol Club is a tax exempt organization dedicated to the shooting sports at Michigan State.

MSU CENTERFIRE is the recreational and action shooting division of the MSU Rifle and Pistol Club. We have always been a fluid entity, changing with the interests of its memebers. This club is simply another iteration.

Wait... Are you guys the same club as the MSU Shooting Sports Club?

No.

We are a different club. We are a separate, non-affiliated entity as their facility does not support the needs of our club.

We are all happy to live on a campus that is so supporting of the shooting sports that there are two clubs.

I see this club frequently uses black rifles, and guns with magazine capacities in excess of 30 rounds. Are these guns safe to use?

Yes.

But let's be clear about one thing: the shooting sports, like any other sports, carry some inherent dangers, which is why we have extensive safety policies in place, and all new members are required to undergo range safety training before handling firearms.

I'm sold. I want to shoot and compete. What equipment should I plan on buying?

Awesome. There are a lot of great products on the market, but making a good selection is based mostly on what you want to do.

ACTS is our most popular, best value event. Their monthly 2-gun matches challenge shooters to run, jump and crawl their way to engage a wide variety of targets with a combination of rifle and pistol shooting. Their are virtually no restrictions on what kind of guns to bring, but you should be looking for semi-automatic, magazine-fed weapons. Both AR-15 and AK-47 platform rifles are ideal. Full size handguns (with 15 round capacity or more) work well for a sidearm, preferably in 9mm caliber, like the Glock 17 or Springfield XD-9.

If TRAP is your game, you'll be looking for a 12ga shotgun, preferably with a 3" chamber. Almost any shotgun will work, but there are some shotguns that are optimized for trap competition.

Before you run to the store, make sure to check with the club. We often have equipment in supply that you can use for practice and competition.

If you want to maximize your firearm dollars, and buy a gun that also functions well in hunting or home defense use, it may influence you buying choice as well. The MSU CENTERFIRE membership and coaching community is a great resource for some good info on the best products to meet your needs. Also, be sure to take advantage of the 10% discount on firearms offered to MSU CENTERFIRE shooters by Alpha Dog Customs.
